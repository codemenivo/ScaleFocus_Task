In number theory, a perfect number is a positive integer

that is equal to the sum of its positive divisors. By this rule,

six is the smallest perfect number.

If you scratched your head and said, "Huh?," here's an

Example to clarify:

1 + 2 + 3 = 6

Write a program to find the first 5/10/15 perfect numbers.
